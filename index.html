Privacy Methods

Method 1: Use a Privacy-Focused Browser

Avoid browsers like Brave and Opera. Both use Chromium, which ties back to Google. My browser of choice is Firefox, which is open-source and committed to privacy. It uses the Gecko engine, ensuring independence and community-driven development.

For search engines, consider alternatives to Google. While it offers convenience, I prefer DuckDuckGo for its !bangs feature, which allows quick navigation to various sites.

Method 2: Encrypt Your Communications

Use end-to-end encrypted messaging apps like Signal or Element (Matrix). These apps ensure that only the sender and receiver can read the messages, making it nearly impossible for third parties to intercept your conversations.

Method 3: Secure Your Devices

Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches. Use strong, unique passwords for all accounts and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible. For extra protection, consider using a password manager to securely store your credentials.
